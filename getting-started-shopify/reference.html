Troubleshooting

Area Issue Symptoms Quick fix
Store settings Legal pages still show old details Policy pages show old address or defaults Update policy pages in Online Store > Pages and save changes.
Payments No payment option at checkout Customers cannot complete payment step In Settings > Payments, confirm at least one provider is active.
Products Products not visible on storefront Catalog appears empty or incomplete Set products to Active and confirm they are available on the Online Store channel.
Shipping No shipping options at checkout Customers cannot continue after address entry Set at least one shipping zone and one shipping rate.
Checkout Checkout feels too long Higher abandonment during checkout flow Reduce required fields in Settings > Checkout.

Glossary

Admin
Shopify backend where you manage products, settings and orders.
Checkout
Flow where customers enter shipping and payment details.
Collection
A group of products displayed together.
Credit card processing fee
Fee charged by the payment processor for card transactions.
Domain
Public web address for your store.
Payment provider
A service that processes customer payments.
Sales channel
Place where products can be sold, such as Online Store or social channels.
Shipping profile
Shipping rules for a specific product group.
Shipping rate
The amount customers pay for shipping.
Shipping zone
A geographic area you ship to.
Transaction fee
Shopify fee applied when using a third party payment provider.
Variant
A version of a product with options such as size or color.

FAQs

How long does setup usually take?
Most new merchants complete the five essential setup areas in 2 to 4 hours across one or more sessions.

Do I need technical skills to use Shopify?
No. This manual is designed for beginners with basic web and form-filling skills.

Can I change settings after launch?
Yes, but changes to address, currency, shipping and policies can affect active operations. Review carefully before changing.

What if Shopify Payments is not available in my country?
Use an alternative provider such as PayPal or Stripe and account for extra transaction costs.

Should I require customer accounts?
Usually no. Optional or guest checkout tends to convert better for most stores.
