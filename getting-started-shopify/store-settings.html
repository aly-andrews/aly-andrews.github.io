Configure store settings

Address, contact details and legal pages.

Store settings form the foundation of a Shopify store. They control business information, contact details and legal pages. Getting this right before launch helps avoid tax, shipping and compliance issues later.

On this page

  • Contact information
  • Store address
  • Terms of service template
  • Policy templates

Contact information

Store contact information is how customers reach you and how you receive order notifications. Keep it up to date so you do not miss important messages. Use an inbox that you monitor regularly.

Steps

  1. In Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. In Store contact information, enter the store email and phone number (optional).
  4. Click Save.

Store address

The store address is used for tax and shipping calculations and appears in legal documents. It is also pulled into policy templates and shipping settings, so set it correctly before creating legal pages or configuring shipping. If you work from home and prefer not to show a home address on customer facing pages, consider a PO Box or business address before generating policy templates.

Steps

  1. In Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. Scroll to Store address and enter the full address.
  4. Click Save.

Terms of service template

Legal pages are required for compliance and help build customer trust. Shopify provides templates for Privacy Policy, Terms of Service and Refund Policy that you can generate and edit. Shipping policies usually need to be written manually. Use Shopify's shipping policy guide for a free template and examples.

Steps

  1. In Shopify admin, go to Online Store > Pages.
  2. Click Add page.
  3. Enter Terms of Service as the title.
  4. Generate a Shopify template or paste your own terms, then click Save.
  5. Go to Settings > Legal, select the page in the Terms of service field and click Save.

Warning

Terms of Service are legally binding. If you are unsure about the language, work with a lawyer or local advisor.

Policy templates

Shopify provides templates for Privacy Policy, Terms of Service and Refund Policy. They are starting points. Review and edit them before publishing because templates can include default values that may not match your business and they pull the store address automatically. Policy pages are legally binding, so update every line to match your actual business practices. A simple checklist can help you track which settings are confirmed before you start taking orders.
