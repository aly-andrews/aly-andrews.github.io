Set up your store settings

Configure your address, contact details, legal pages and basic store details.

Your store settings form the foundation of your Shopify store. They control your business information, contact details and legal pages. Completing this work accurately before launch helps you avoid tax, shipping and compliance issues later.

On this page

  • Adding your store address
  • Configuring your store details
  • Creating your legal pages
  • Verifying your store settings

Adding your store address

Your store address is used for tax and shipping calculations and appears in legal documents. Setting this correctly at the start prevents problems later.

Tip

If you work from home and do not want your home address on customer facing pages, consider using a PO Box or business address before you generate policy templates.

Adding your address

Follow these steps to add your store address:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. Scroll to the Store address section.
  4. Enter your complete address, including street, city, state or province, postal or ZIP code and country.
  5. Click Save.

Note

Your store address is pulled into multiple places, including policy templates and shipping settings. Set it correctly before you create legal pages or configure shipping.

Setting your timezone and currency

Follow these steps to set your store timezone and currency:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. In the Store currency section, choose your store currency.
  4. In the Timezone section, choose the timezone where your business operates.
  5. Click Save.

Configuring your contact information

Follow these steps to configure your contact information:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. In Store contact information, enter your store email address.
  4. Enter a store phone number (optional but recommended).
  5. Click Save.

Note

This email receives order notifications and customer questions. Use an inbox that you monitor regularly.

Warning

Changing currency after you add products can affect pricing. Set your currency before you start adding products.

Configuring your store details

Store details include your store name, description and branding elements such as your logo. These appear across your storefront and help customers understand who you are.

Setting your store name and description

Follow these steps to set your store name and description:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. In the Store details section, enter your Store name.
  4. Enter a short Store description that explains what you sell.
  5. Click Save.

Tip

Your store description can appear in search results. Keep it clear, specific and focused on what you sell.

Uploading your store logo

Follow these steps to upload your store logo:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. In the Store logo section, click Upload or drag and drop your logo image.
  4. Adjust the logo size if a preview is shown.
  5. Click Save.

Tip

Use a high quality image file, ideally a PNG with a transparent background, that is still readable at small sizes.

Creating your legal pages

Legal pages are required for compliance and help build customer trust. Many jurisdictions expect privacy policies, terms of service and refund policies for online businesses.

Creating your Privacy Policy page

Follow these steps to create your Privacy Policy page:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Online Store > Pages.
  2. Click Add page.
  3. Enter Privacy Policy as the page title.
  4. In the content area, either generate a Shopify template or paste your own policy content.
  5. Click Save.
  6. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings > Legal.
  7. In the Privacy policy field, select your Privacy Policy page.
  8. Click Save.

Warning

Shopify templates are starting points only. Review and edit them so they match your actual business practices before you publish.

Creating your Terms of Service page

Follow these steps to create your Terms of Service page:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Online Store > Pages.
  2. Click Add page.
  3. Enter Terms of Service (or Terms and Conditions) as the page title.
  4. In the content area, either generate a Shopify template or paste your own terms.
  5. Click Save.
  6. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings > Legal.
  7. In the Terms of service field, select your Terms of Service page.
  8. Click Save.

Warning

Your Terms of Service are legally binding. If you are unsure about the language, work with a lawyer or local advisor.

Creating your Refund Policy page

Follow these steps to create your Refund Policy page:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Online Store > Pages.
  2. Click Add page.
  3. Enter Refund Policy as the page title.
  4. In the content area, either generate a Shopify template or paste your own refund policy.
  5. Click Save.
  6. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings > Legal.
  7. In the Refund policy field, select your Refund Policy page.
  8. Click Save.

Warning

Make sure the refund window and conditions in your policy match what you actually offer. If a template says “30 days” and you only offer 14, change it before you publish.

Verifying your store settings

Before you move on to payment setup, verify that your store settings and legal pages are complete and accurate.

Reviewing your store settings

Follow these steps to review your store settings:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click General.
  3. Confirm that your store address, contact information, currency, timezone, store name and logo are all correct.
  4. Click Save if you make any changes.
  5. Go to Settings > Legal.
  6. Confirm that Privacy Policy, Terms of Service and Refund Policy pages are all selected.
  7. Open each policy link and skim the content to make sure details match your business.

About policy templates

Shopify usually provides templates for Privacy Policy, Terms of Service and Refund Policy. These templates are starting points and must be reviewed before publishing.

  • Templates can include default values that may not match your business.
  • Templates can pull your store address automatically.
  • Shipping policies usually need to be written manually.

Warning

Policy pages are legally binding. Review every template line and update values to match your actual business practices.

Tip

Create a simple checklist to track which settings you have confirmed. It is easier to fix issues now than after you start taking orders.
