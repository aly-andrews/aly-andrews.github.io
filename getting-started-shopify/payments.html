Configure your payments

Set up your payment provider so customers can complete checkout and place orders.

Payment setup is essential because customers cannot place orders without an active payment provider. Start by checking what providers are available in your region, then activate Shopify Payments or an alternative provider.

On this page

  • Understanding provider options
  • Setting up Shopify Payments
  • Setting up an alternative provider

Understanding provider options

Choose a payment setup based on your region, business model and fee structure. Shopify Payments is often the simplest option when available.

Check provider restrictions early if you sell regulated or high risk products.

Checking available payment providers

Follow these steps to check available payment providers:

Desktop

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Payments.
  3. Review the Payment providers section.
  4. Review available third party options in your region.

Setting up Shopify Payments

If Shopify Payments is available in your region, it is usually the fastest way to start accepting payments.

Activating Shopify Payments

Follow these steps to activate Shopify Payments:

Desktop

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Payments.
  3. In Shopify Payments, click Complete account setup.
  4. Enter your business and identity details.
  5. Review terms and click Submit for review.
Business verification can take one to three business days depending on your country and business type.

Setting up an alternative provider

If Shopify Payments is not available in your region, you can activate providers such as PayPal, Stripe or other local gateways.

Activating PayPal

Follow these steps to activate PayPal:

Desktop

  1. Create or verify your PayPal Business account.
  2. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  3. Click Payments.
  4. In the PayPal section, click Set up.
  5. Enter your PayPal account email and click Activate.
