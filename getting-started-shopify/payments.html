Configure your payments

Set up your payment provider so customers can complete checkout and place orders.

Payment setup is essential because customers cannot place orders without an active payment provider. Start by checking what providers are available in your region, then activate Shopify Payments or an alternative provider.

Why Payment Setup Matters

  • Customers cannot complete checkout without an active payment provider.
  • Provider availability depends on region and what you sell.
  • Fee structure directly affects pricing and margin strategy.
  • Verification requirements can delay launch if started too late.

Understanding provider options

Choose a payment setup based on your region, business model and fee structure. Shopify Payments is often the simplest option when available.

Payment provider landscape

Before you decide, compare your options:

  • Shopify Payments: Built into Shopify admin, no Shopify transaction fees and unified reporting.
  • Alternative providers: Options such as PayPal, Stripe or regional gateways that may have different features or regional availability.

Warning

Check provider restrictions early if you sell regulated or high risk products.

Checking available payment providers

Follow these steps to check available payment providers:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Payments.
  3. Review the Payment providers section.
  4. Review available third party options in your region.

Understanding payment fees

New merchants often confuse transaction fees with credit card processing fees. Understanding this difference helps you set accurate product pricing before launch.

  • Transaction fees: Charged by Shopify when you use a third party provider instead of Shopify Payments.
  • Credit card processing fees: Charged by the payment processor for each card transaction.

Example: If you use Shopify Payments, you usually pay only card processing fees. If you use a third party provider, you may pay both Shopify transaction fees and provider processing fees.

Tip

Build payment fees into your pricing model early so margins stay healthy as orders grow.

Understanding provider restrictions

Payment providers can restrict what you sell. Review restrictions before launch, especially if you sell regulated or high risk products.

  • High risk categories: Some supplements, CBD, adult content and other restricted categories.
  • Regional restrictions: Provider availability and terms vary by country.
  • Business model restrictions: Some service types or fulfillment models may require extra review.

Setting up Shopify Payments

If Shopify Payments is available in your region, it is usually the fastest way to start accepting payments.

Why merchants choose Shopify Payments

  • No additional Shopify transaction fees.
  • Native setup inside Shopify admin.
  • Centralized payment reporting with orders and payouts.

Activating Shopify Payments

Follow these steps to activate Shopify Payments:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Payments.
  3. In Shopify Payments, click Complete account setup.
  4. Enter your business and identity details.
  5. Review terms and click Submit for review.

Result: Your Shopify Payments application is submitted for review.

Note

Business verification can take one to three business days depending on your country and business type.

Completing business verification

Completing business verification

Follow these steps to complete Shopify Payments verification:

  1. Check your email for verification requests from Shopify Payments.
  2. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings > Payments.
  3. Open any pending request and review required documents.
  4. Upload requested business, banking and identity documents.
  5. Wait for review and monitor status updates.

Result: Once verification is complete, you can begin accepting payments with Shopify Payments.

Tip

Prepare documents in advance: business registration records, bank account documentation and valid owner identification.

Understanding Shopify Payments fees

Shopify Payments credit card processing rates vary by plan and transaction type. Check current Shopify pricing for your region before finalizing product prices.

  • Basic Shopify: Higher processing rate than upper plans.
  • Shopify: Mid tier processing rate.
  • Advanced Shopify: Lower processing rate for higher volume stores.

Setting up an alternative provider

If Shopify Payments is not available in your region, you can activate providers such as PayPal, Stripe or other local gateways.

Alternative provider overview

Common alternatives include PayPal, Stripe, Square and regional providers. Compare setup requirements, support quality and total fee impact before choosing.

Activating PayPal

Follow these steps to activate PayPal:

  1. Create or verify your PayPal Business account.
  2. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  3. Click Payments.
  4. In the PayPal section, click Set up.
  5. Enter your PayPal account email and click Activate.

Note

With third party providers such as PayPal, you may pay both Shopify transaction fees and provider processing fees.
