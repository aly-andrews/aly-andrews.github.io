Configure payments

Payment provider and fee structure.

Customers cannot place orders without an active payment provider. Provider availability depends on region and what you sell. Fee structure affects pricing and margin, and verification can delay launch if started too late. Check what is available in your region, then activate Shopify Payments or an alternative.

Provider details

Provider options

Choose a payment setup based on region, business model and fee structure. Shopify Payments is built into the admin, has no Shopify transaction fees and unified reporting. Alternatives such as PayPal, Stripe or regional gateways offer different features and availability. Check provider restrictions early if you sell regulated or high risk products such as some supplements, CBD or adult content. Availability and terms vary by country.

Payment fees

Transaction fees are charged by Shopify when you use a third party provider instead of Shopify Payments. Credit card processing fees are charged by the processor for each card transaction. With Shopify Payments you usually pay only processing fees. With a third party provider you may pay both. Build fees into your pricing early so margins stay healthy.

Provider restrictions

Providers can restrict what you sell. Review restrictions before launch. High risk categories, regional rules and business model requirements may need extra review.

Shopify Payments

When available in your region, Shopify Payments is usually the fastest option. It has no Shopify transaction fees, native setup and centralized reporting. Processing rates vary by plan. Check current pricing before finalizing product prices.

Steps: Activate Shopify Payments

  1. In Shopify admin, go to Settings > Payments.
  2. In Shopify Payments, click Complete account setup.
  3. Enter business and identity details, review terms and click Submit for review.

Steps: Business verification

  1. Check email for verification requests from Shopify Payments.
  2. Go to Settings > Payments and open any pending request.
  3. Upload business, banking and identity documents and wait for review.

Note

Verification can take one to three business days. Prepare business registration, bank documents and ID in advance.

Alternative provider

When Shopify Payments is not available, use providers such as PayPal, Stripe or regional gateways. Compare setup requirements, support and total fee impact. With third party providers you may pay both Shopify transaction fees and provider processing fees.

Steps: Activate PayPal

  1. Create or verify a PayPal Business account.
  2. In Shopify admin go to Settings > Payments.
  3. In the PayPal section, click Set up, enter account email and click Activate.
