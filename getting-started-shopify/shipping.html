Set up your shipping

Create shipping zones and rates so shipping calculations work correctly at checkout.

Shipping setup determines where you ship and how much customers pay. Start with one domestic zone, add weight based rates and test your checkout flow with sample orders.

Why Shipping Setup Matters

  • Checkout cannot complete without shipping zones and rates.
  • Shipping logic can directly affect profitability.
  • Incorrect setup can block orders in specific regions.

Understanding zones, rates and profiles

Why this matters: checkout cannot complete without valid zones and rates, and poor shipping configuration can erase margin quickly.

  • Shipping zones define where you ship.
  • Shipping rates define how much customers pay.
  • Shipping profiles define different shipping rules for different product groups.

Shipping rate types

  • Weight based: Most reliable when products vary in weight.
  • Price based: Simple but risky for heavy low price products.
  • Flat rate: Works best when products have similar shipping cost.

Understanding shipping profiles

Most new merchants can start with the default shipping profile. Add custom profiles later if different product groups need different shipping rates.

Overview: When to Use Shipping Profiles

Use additional shipping profiles when product groups have meaningfully different shipping costs, fulfillment locations or delivery expectations.

Procedure: Understanding Default Shipping Profile

Follow these steps to review your default profile:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Shipping and delivery.
  3. Open Shipping profiles.
  4. Select Default shipping profile to inspect current zones and rates.

Creating a domestic shipping zone

Overview: Domestic Shipping Considerations

Before you configure rates, estimate actual carrier costs, packaging cost and average order weight so your pricing model is realistic.

Creating your domestic shipping zone

Follow these steps to create your domestic shipping zone:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Shipping and delivery.
  3. In Shipping zones, click Add shipping zone if needed.
  4. Enter a zone name and select your country or region.
  5. Click Done.

Result: Customers in this zone can now see shipping options at checkout once rates are added.

Adding weight based shipping rates

Adding weight based rates

Follow these steps to add weight based shipping rates:

  1. Open your domestic shipping zone.
  2. In Shipping rates, click Add rate.
  3. Select Weight based as the rate type.
  4. Enter weight ranges and prices.
  5. Click Save.

Result: Checkout now calculates shipping cost based on total order weight.

Warning

Accurate product weight is required for accurate shipping charges at checkout.

Testing shipping calculations

Testing shipping at checkout

Follow these steps to test your shipping setup:

  1. Go to your storefront and add one or more products to cart.
  2. Proceed to checkout and enter a shipping address in your active zone.
  3. Confirm shipping options and rates appear correctly.
  4. Test a second address for another zone if you ship internationally.

Tip

Test lightweight and heavyweight orders separately to confirm your rate logic matches real fulfillment costs.
