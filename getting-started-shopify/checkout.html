Configure your checkout

Set account and field requirements so checkout stays simple and conversion friendly.

Checkout is the final step before purchase. Keep it simple, require only essential fields and test the flow from cart to payment confirmation.

Why Checkout Configuration Matters

  • Checkout is the final conversion point before purchase.
  • Mobile checkout quality strongly affects completed orders.
  • Overly strict requirements can increase abandonment.

Understanding checkout options

Checkout settings control customer account requirements, customer information fields and order processing behavior.

Overview: Checkout Customization Areas

  • Customer accounts: Optional, required or disabled account creation.
  • Customer information: Required and optional checkout fields.
  • Order processing: Post purchase behavior and notifications.
  • Visual controls: Limited branding controls depending on plan.

Accessing checkout settings

Opening checkout settings

Follow these steps to open checkout settings:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Checkout.
  3. Review sections for customer accounts, customer information and order processing.

Configuring customer account settings

Account settings affect conversion. In most cases, optional or guest checkout performs better than requiring account creation before purchase.

Configuring account requirements

Follow these steps to configure customer account settings:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Checkout.
  3. In Customer accounts, choose optional, required or disabled.
  4. Click Save.

Customizing checkout fields

Overview: Required vs Optional Fields

Require only information you need for fulfillment and payment. Extra required fields usually reduce completion rates.

Setting required and optional fields

Follow these steps to customize checkout fields:

  1. In your Shopify admin, go to Settings.
  2. Click Checkout.
  3. In Customer information, choose which fields are required or optional.
  4. Enable only the fields you need for fulfillment.
  5. Click Save.

Result: Customers see only the fields you configured, which can reduce checkout friction.

Tip

Requiring fewer fields can reduce checkout friction and cart abandonment.

Testing checkout flow

Testing checkout on desktop and mobile

Follow these steps to test checkout before launch:

  1. Add a product to cart and proceed to checkout.
  2. Confirm required fields, shipping options and payment methods appear correctly.
  3. Repeat the same flow on a mobile device.
  4. Complete a test order and verify it appears in Orders.

Best practices

  • Keep guest checkout available unless your business model requires accounts.
  • Require only fields needed to fulfill and verify orders.
  • Retest checkout after any shipping, payment or account-setting changes.

Warning

Always test checkout on a real phone before launch. Mobile issues can significantly affect conversion.
